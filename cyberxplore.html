Cyber Xplore Cybersecurity Workshop

Event Details

Date: 9th January 2025

Venue: Sri Manakula Vinayagar Engineering College

Organized by: Starfort

Participants: 500 Engineering Students

Objective of the Workshop

The Cyber Xplore workshop aimed to raise awareness of critical cybersecurity issues and equip students with practical knowledge to safeguard themselves against cyber threats.

In today’s digital age, the increasing number of cybercrimes and data breaches make it crucial for future professionals, especially engineering students, to be well-versed in cybersecurity fundamentals. The workshop’s objective was to bridge the gap between academic knowledge and real-world cybersecurity challenges, providing students with both theoretical background and practical tools to tackle risks in the digital world.

Participants

The workshop was attended by a total of 500 engineering students from various departments and academic years at Sri Manakula Vinayagar Engineering College. The diverse group of participants reflected the growing importance of cybersecurity knowledge across all engineering fields.

Students actively engaged throughout the workshop, contributing to its success through participation and interaction.

Workshop Agenda and Key Topics Covered

Introduction and Ice-Breaking Session

The workshop began with an interactive ice-breaking activity designed to set a positive tone for the session. Students participated in polls, quizzes, and short challenges using their mobile phones to assess their understanding of cybersecurity.

This activity helped the presenters gauge the participants’ existing knowledge while encouraging active engagement from the beginning of the workshop.

Biometrics, VPN, Cookies, Social Media Privacy and AI-Driven Data

The workshop then transitioned into discussions on common technologies and digital privacy practices including their advantages and potential risks.

  • Biometric Authentication: Discussion on biometric security systems such as fingerprint recognition, facial recognition and retina scans including their strengths and limitations.
  • Virtual Private Networks (VPNs): Explanation of how VPNs protect user privacy and secure internet communication along with risks associated with unreliable or free VPN services.
  • Cookies and Online Tracking: Understanding how cookies function in online browsing and how they may be used for tracking user activity.
  • Social Media Privacy: The importance of protecting personal information on social media platforms and the risks of oversharing sensitive details.
  • AI-Driven Data Collection: Discussion on how artificial intelligence is used for data analysis and cybersecurity along with privacy concerns.

Advanced Cybersecurity Topics

The workshop progressed to more advanced cybersecurity topics including:

  • Social Engineering
  • Phishing Attacks
  • Malware

Real-World Case Studies

The workshop also examined real cybersecurity incidents including major data breaches affecting organizations.

  • Rockstar Data Breach
  • SBI Data Breach

Practical Demonstration Session

The workshop included live demonstrations showing how cyberattacks occur in real-world scenarios including social engineering, phishing and malware attacks.

Social Engineering Demonstration

A participant from the audience was selected and a casual interaction was used to demonstrate how attackers can extract sensitive information through psychological manipulation.

  • Avoid sharing personal information without verification
  • Be cautious when interacting with unknown individuals
  • Verify authenticity before providing information

Phishing Demonstration

The phishing demonstration explained several attack techniques:

  • Email Spoofing
  • Link Masking
  • Location Tracking

Participants learned how attackers disguise malicious links and how phishing attacks can steal credentials or gather location data.

Malware Demonstration

A malware simulation demonstrated how attackers gain unauthorized access to systems using penetration testing tools such as Metasploit and Hoxshell.

  • Unauthorized system access
  • Monitoring user activity
  • Accessing sensitive files
  • Remote control of system resources

Key Outcomes and Learnings

  • Improved awareness of cybersecurity threats
  • Understanding how human behavior can be exploited
  • Learning practical defensive cybersecurity practices

Student Engagement and Feedback

Students showed strong engagement throughout the workshop through interactive discussions and Q&A sessions. Many participants expressed interest in learning more about cybersecurity and exploring career opportunities in the field.

Impact of the Workshop

The Cyber Xplore workshop significantly improved cybersecurity awareness among engineering students and helped them understand how cyberattacks occur and how to protect themselves in the digital world.

Conclusion

The Cyber Xplore workshop organized by Starfort successfully delivered valuable cybersecurity knowledge to more than 500 engineering students through interactive discussions, demonstrations and real-world case studies.
