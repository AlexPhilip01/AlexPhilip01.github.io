02 Objective

The Cyberxplore workshop aimed to raise awareness of critical cybersecurity issues and equip engineering students with practical knowledge to safeguard themselves against cyber threats in the digital age.

With the increasing frequency of cybercrimes and data breaches, it is essential for future professionals across all engineering disciplines to develop a solid understanding of cybersecurity fundamentals. The workshop was designed to bridge the gap between academic knowledge and real-world security challenges — providing students with both theoretical grounding and practical tools to navigate risks in modern digital environments.

03 Workshop Agenda

Introduction & Ice-Breaking Session
The workshop opened with an interactive ice-breaking activity using mobile-based polls, quizzes, and short challenges to assess participants' existing cybersecurity knowledge while setting a positive, engaging tone.
Digital Privacy & Modern Technologies
Discussion of common technologies and their privacy implications — biometrics, VPNs, cookies, social media, and AI-driven data collection — covering both advantages and potential risks.
Advanced Cybersecurity Concepts
Progression to advanced topics including social engineering, phishing attacks, and malware categories — equipping students to recognise and respond to real-world attack techniques.
Real-World Case Studies
Analysis of high-profile incidents including the Rockstar Data Breach and SBI Data Breach — demonstrating the real-world impact of cyberattacks on organisations and individuals.
Live Demonstration Session
Hands-on demonstrations of social engineering, phishing, and malware attack scenarios to show how attackers exploit both human and technical vulnerabilities in practice.
Q&A & Discussion
Open Q&A session with active student participation, discussions on cybersecurity career paths, and guidance on further learning resources.

04 Topics Covered

🔐
Biometric Authentication
Security systems including fingerprint, facial recognition, and retina scans — strengths, limitations, and real-world risks.
🌐
VPNs & Online Privacy
How VPNs protect user privacy and secure internet communication, plus risks of unreliable or free VPN services.
🍪
Cookies & Tracking
How cookies function in online browsing and how they are used for tracking user activity across websites.
📱
Social Media Privacy
Protecting personal information on social platforms and understanding the risks of oversharing sensitive details.
🤖
AI & Data Collection
How artificial intelligence is used for data analysis and cybersecurity, along with associated privacy concerns.
🎭
Social Engineering
Psychological manipulation techniques used by attackers to extract confidential information from unsuspecting individuals.
🎣
Phishing Attacks
Phishing email design, spoofed links, credential theft techniques, and how to identify and avoid them.
🦠
Malware Types
Viruses, trojans, ransomware, and spyware — how each operates, spreads, and what defences apply.

05 Live Demonstrations

The workshop included practical, live demonstrations of real-world attack scenarios — one of the most impactful components of the session, helping participants connect theory to attacker behaviour.

🎭
Social Engineering Demo
Human Manipulation · Psychological Attack

A live volunteer from the audience participated in a conversation-based demonstration showing how attackers build trust and extract personal information through psychological manipulation — without any technical tools.

  • Demonstrated how attackers establish rapport to lower defences
  • Showed how sensitive details are extracted conversationally
  • Key lesson: never share personal information without verification
🎣
Phishing Demo
Email Spoofing · Credential Theft · Link Masking

Participants were shown how attackers craft convincing phishing emails and fake login pages to steal user credentials — including techniques to disguise malicious intent.

  • Email spoofing to impersonate trusted senders
  • Link masking to disguise malicious URLs behind legitimate-looking text
  • Location tracking using embedded malicious links
🦠
Malware Demo
Metasploit · Hoxshell · Remote Access

A simulated malware attack demonstrated how attackers gain unauthorized remote access to systems using tools such as Metasploit and Hoxshell — illustrating the capabilities available to modern threat actors.

  • Unauthorized system access via exploit frameworks
  • Monitoring user activity without detection
  • Accessing sensitive files and documents remotely
  • Full remote control of system resources

06 Key Outcomes

The workshop achieved measurable improvements in participant awareness and generated strong interest in cybersecurity as a career path across all participating departments.

🧠
Improved awareness of modern cybersecurity threats among 650+ engineering students
🔍
Practical understanding of how human behaviour can be exploited by attackers
🛡️
Knowledge of defensive security practices to protect personal and professional digital assets
Student Engagement: Participants actively engaged in discussions and Q&A sessions throughout the workshop. Many expressed strong interest in pursuing cybersecurity further — both as a skill set and as a career.

07 Conclusion

The Cyberxplore workshop organised by Starfort successfully delivered practical cybersecurity knowledge to 650 engineering students at Sri Manakula Vinayagar Engineering College through interactive discussions, real-world case studies, and live attack demonstrations.

The session demonstrated that cybersecurity awareness is not limited to specialised disciplines — it is a critical skill for all engineers operating in today's interconnected digital landscape. By combining foundational concepts with hands-on demonstrations, the workshop made cybersecurity tangible, relevant, and actionable for every participant.

Impact: The workshop significantly improved cybersecurity awareness among participants and equipped students with the knowledge needed to protect themselves and their future organisations from evolving cyber threats.
